Interactive Map Analysis of the Coronavirus Cases in NYC

Description: The primary goal is to create a web mapping with a listing of all of the people living in New York City. Using Data mapping to showcase the affected people in New York City by the zip code. I used leaflet a javascript library.


Article

As of Thursday night, there have been more than 332,931 confirmed cases of the coronavirus in New York, including more than 185,653 in New York City. More than 26,206 people with COVID-19 have died in the state, including people with probable, but not confirmed, cases of the virus. Nationwide, over 1.2 million cases of COVID-19 have been confirmed across all 50 states, Washington, D.C., and three U.S. territories. More than 75,744 Americans have lost their lives to the virus. COVID-19 has killed more than 269,584 worldwide — and far more in the U.S. than in any other country. Inside the recently opened Kimmel Pavilion in Manhattan, hundreds of patients rest in rooms designed to minimize infection. When the nurses do take a break, a soundtrack of wailing sirens reminds them what lies ahead on their return. On March 10, N.Y.U.’s medical center had just two coronavirus patients. Today, patients are spread over every floor of both Kimmel and nearby Tisch Hospital, said Robert Magyar, a hospital spokesman. The medical staff is weary, but many said they were relieved to have the supplies and protective gear that are in short supply at so many other institutions.
